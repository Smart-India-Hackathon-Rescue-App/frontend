Preventive measures for Fire Hazards



1. Good Housekeeping: Keep your workplace or home tidy. Regularly remove combustible waste, including accumulations of dust.

2. Control Ignition Sources: Keep ignition sources away from combustible material or flammable liquids and gases.

3. Emergency Planning: Be prepared for an emergency, such as a fire. An emergency plan should clearly explain what to do in the event of an emergency.

4. Fire Alarms and Detectors: Provide a method for detecting a fire quickly and raising the alarm. The warning system should be clearly heard throughout the premises.

5. Escape Routes: All escape routes must be easily identifiable, with instructions about the means of escape displayed.

6. Regular Inspection and Maintenance: Regular inspection and maintenance of electrical systems and practicing safe usage of electrical appliances can help prevent electrical fires.

7. Smoke Alarms: Install smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside bedrooms and outside sleeping areas. Test smoke alarms every month.

8. Fire Escape Plan: Talk with all family members about a fire escape plan and practice the plan twice a year.

Remember, if a fire occurs in your home, GET OUT, STAY OUT and CALL FOR HELP.
