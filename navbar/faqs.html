Frequently Asked Questions



Q: What is disaster management?
A: Disaster management is the process of planning, organizing, co-ordinating, and implementing measures to prevent, mitigate, prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters, emergencies, and crises.
Q: What is the role of Government in disaster management?
A: Governments at various levels (local, state, and federal) play a critical role in coordinating disaster response, providing resources, and disseminating information.
Q: How can I stay informed during a disaster?
A: Stay informed through emergency alerts, local news, weather apps, and social media channels of relevant authorities.
Q: How do I lodge a complaint?
A: The grievances can be lodged online. In cases where internet facility is not available or even otherwise, the citizen is free to send her/his grievance by Post. There is no prescribed format. The grievance may be written on any plain sheet of paper or on a Postcard / Inland letter.
Q: What is the role of technology in disaster management?
A: Technology aids in communication, early warning systems, and data collection, which are crucial for effective disaster management.
Q: How can I access emergency services during a disaster?
A: Dial emergency numbers for immediate assistance. Save these numbers in your phone and know how to use them.
Q: How can I get involved in disaster preparedness and response efforts in my community?
A: Contact local volunteer organizations, attend community meetings, and take disaster preparedness training courses.
Q: What is the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)?
A:The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) is a statutory body established under the Disaster Management Act, 2005, responsible for formulating policies, plans, and guidelines for disaster management in India.
Q:What is the significance of the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF)?
A:The National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) is an Indian specialized force constituted "for the purpose of special response to a threatening disaster situation or disaster" under the Disaster Management Act, 2005.
