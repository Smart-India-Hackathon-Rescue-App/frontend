Precautions to take against avalanches and stay safe:


1. Get the forecast, training, and gear: The most important thing when it comes to avalanche safety is to get the forecast, get the training, and get the gear.

2. Educate yourself on warning signs: Consider taking an avalanche education course if you spend time in the backcountry during snowy conditionsÂ¹. Know the red flags signaling an impending avalanche.

3. Be aware of current and future weather conditions: Rain, strong sun, or warming temperatures increase avalanche risk.

4. Preparation: Before venturing into snowy wilderness areas, take the extra time to prepare for the conditions, by checking forecasts and acquiring safety gear.

5. Buddy system: Travel with others and communicate with each other about your observations to make informed decisions.

6. Use and carry safety equipment and rescue gear: If your partner or others are buried, call 9-1-1 and then begin to search if it is safe to do so.

7. Take first aid training: Recognize and treat suffocation, hypothermia, traumatic injury, and shock.

8. Travel with a guide: travelling with a loacl guide is safer as they know the area better and will help us to avoid dagerous places.

9. Follow avalanche warnings on roads: Roads may be closed or vehicles may be advised not to stop on the roadside.
