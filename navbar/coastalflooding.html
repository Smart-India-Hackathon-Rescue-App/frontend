Here are some precautions you can take to protect yourself and your property from coastal flooding:

What To Do During a Flood?

1. Elevate your home from the base while constructing to avoid entry of floodwater or construct walls to stop it.
2. Install "check valves" in sewer pipes to prevent floodwater from entering the drains of your home.
3. If your home is in very low land, then shift to a safer place and keep all furniture at an elevated level.
4. Keep all your documents in a safe water-proof bag.
5. Keep updated with flood safety tips given by the Government.
6. Know the safest route of escape to the nearest shelter.
7. Install the electricity supplying switches and plugs above a certain level from the floor, which is most unlikely to get reached by the floodwater.
8. Keep all your electronic devices in secure water proof bags.

What Not to Do During a Flood?

1. Do not go in the flood-prone zone or leave your home if there is any flood forecast.
2. Take all valuables with you and do not keep any valuables at home as you have to evacuate the place.
3. Do not ignore advice, messages, or instructions given by Government and local municipal bodies.
4. Do not litter plastics, garbage, or any blockage making materials in the drains.

Safety Precautions to Follow if Flood Is Likely to Hit Your Area

1. Keep calm and updated with news broadcast channels.
2. Be aware of the flash flood.
3. Take all flood safety measures.
4. Do not enter flood-prone areas as precautions during floods.
5. Keep the drainage system of your house clean and out of any blockage.
6. Unplug electrical gadgets and machinery and keep away from the reach of water.
7. Store enough food and drinking water with you.
8. Turn off gas valves and connected devices.
